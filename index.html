Excel Training Program & Digital Workflow Prototype

Executive Summary

This project aims to develop a prototype for digitizing our company's data and workflows, beginning with Excel training and centralized data management. The objective is to increase productivity and enhance problem-solving capabilities across teams—particularly architects and related departments—who regularly use Excel but often lack structured tools or sufficient knowledge to utilize it effectively in their daily operations.

Project Objectives

  • Develop a prototype system using Excel to support internal data handling.
  • Build a training program focused on improving workflow through better Excel use.
  • Identify inefficiencies and bottlenecks, especially in areas like requirement comparisons and area size projections.
  • Deliver targeted Excel training to architecture and supporting departments.
  • Measure improvements in workflow and data accuracy following training and tool implementation.

Project Scope

This project focuses on the design and delivery of a structured Excel training program tailored for architects and related departments. The scope includes:

  • Curriculum Development: Create a training syllabus covering key Excel concepts ranging from data cleaning and logic functions to pivot tables and basic dashboards.
  • Use-Case Alignment: Integrate real-world scenarios into the curriculum (e.g., requirement comparison, area projections, cost tracking) to ensure training relevance.
  • Workshop Delivery: Conduct hands-on training sessions (live or self-paced) designed to improve everyday efficiency and task accuracy.
  • Workflow Assessment: Identify recurring workflow issues Excel can address, and prototype spreadsheet tools or templates to solve them.
  • Exclusions: This scope does not cover database implementation or external system integration at this stage.

Deliverables

  • Excel Training Curriculum: A structured syllabus with learning outcomes, modules, and exercises.
  • Use-Case Worksheets: Functional Excel tools that directly implement relevant workflow use cases, such as requirement comparison and area projection calculators. These tools are intended for daily use, not just practice.
  • Note: While only requirement comparison and area projection calculators will be developed in this project, successful adoption may lead to future expansions such as cost tracking sheets or submission log trackers.
  • Workshop Materials: Slide decks, live demo files, and step-by-step guides.
  • Workflow Issue Report: Document summarizing recurring inefficiencies identified during the training.
  • Evaluation & Feedback Summary: Compilation of participant feedback and training effectiveness metrics.

Applications

  • Less reliance on manual file checking and visual comparisons
  • Reusable templates for budgeting, comparison, and reporting
  • Cross-team data sharing through structured Excel tools

Implementation Plan

  • Phase 1: Training rollout (week 1–3)
  • Phase 2: Collect feedback, measure impact, refine prototype (week 4–6)

Conclusion & Discussion

This project lays the groundwork for larger-scale digital transformation.

By equipping team members with Excel skills and simple prototypes, we can establish a data-driven foundation that can be expanded with future tools (e.g., databases, dashboards, automation).

Further evaluation will determine whether to migrate from Excel to more robust systems (e.g., web-based apps or integrated databases) once workflows are mapped and improved.
